Taj Mahal

The Symbol of Eternal Love in Agra, India

About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a breathtaking ivory-white marble mausoleum built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Constructed between 1632 and 1653, it stands as a symbol of eternal love and architectural brilliance. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is admired for its symmetrical design, intricate carvings, and the stunning gardens that surround it.
